The Problem: Digital Gaming's Trust Deficit
In today's digital gaming landscape, competitive players face a fundamental crisis of trust. Traditional online gaming platforms suffer from:

Centralized Control: Game companies hold absolute power over tournaments and prize distribution

High Barrier to Entry: Professional gaming requires connections, sponsorships, and geographic luck

Trust Issues: Players struggle to verify fair play and secure prize distribution

Financial Exclusion: 99% of skilled players cannot monetize their abilities

Platform Dependency: Games live and die with company decisions

We witnessed this firsthand when our team's favorite competitive game suddenly shut down its tournament system, leaving dedicated players with nothing to show for thousands of hours of practice. The realization hit hard: players don't truly own their skills, their achievements, or their competitive value.

Drawing inspiration from historical chess clubs where any player could challenge another for stakes, we asked: why can't digital gaming capture this pure, peer-to-peer competitive spirit?
